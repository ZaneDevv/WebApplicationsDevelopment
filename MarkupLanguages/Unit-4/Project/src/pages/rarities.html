Gödel's incompleteness theorems

Biology stands for chemistry, chemistry satands for physics and physics stands for mathematics. All mathematics are built up from axioms, statements that are too obvius and are considered objectively true. Though, Kurt Gödel has proven the two Gödel's incompleteness theorems which states that any system must be sustained by statements that cannot be proven in the system. This is huge, because it shows that even the most fundamental part of sciencie may be wrong and we do not have the tools to check it.

Borde-Guth-Vilenkin theorem

In 2003, three american scientists proved what we call now the BGV theorem. This theorem shows that, given a universe in continuous expansion, there must was a moment when thr spacetime was a singularity and, tending to that singularity, all the math we now to describe it breaks down.

Black holes

Black holes are probably the most unknown and mysterious object we know so far. It is quite weird because they are a point in the spacetime that has infinite mass and not even light can scape from it due to its massive gravity. They are quite simple as they can be described using only 3 properties of it.
There are theories about then such that they can be a sort of "portal" bewteen universe since the big bang we know has super similar properties to the birth of a black hole.

The quantum world

A few years ago, scientists like Werner Heisenberg, Albert Einstein or Erwin Scrödinger among others studied a brand new world that extrange and uncomprehensible that those scientists rejected the idea of the existence of that world. Nonetheless, since then, all experiments done prove them wrong: the particles behave as particles and waves at the same time, particle are not defined when nothing is observing them, two particles can communicate instantaneously eventhough they are thousand of light-years of distance.
In our world, it looks like a nonsense, but we cannot forgot that this world and laws make everything we are made of.

Quantum inmortality

One of the interpretations of quantum mechanics is the theory of the many worlds, which states that when a particle is forced to be defined, new universes are created, every single one with a possible result of it, and we are moved to one of those universes. Thus, what would happen when we are about to die? If the have more changes but die, this would creates more universes where we are dead and others where we are not. You, as a conscious, must go to one of them, but you cannot go to one where you are dead, you must go to one where you are alive and the cycle repeats. That could make us sort of "inmortal".
